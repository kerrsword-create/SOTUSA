Common questions

Everything you need to know.

How many lifetime founding memberships are available? +
Exactly 100. We are capping lifetime memberships permanently to preserve the exclusive, uncrowded experience our members expect — a maximum of 62.5 members per lane versus 156 at Scottsdale Gun Club. Thirty-one have already been claimed at the founding rate. Once filled, lifetime memberships will not reopen at this pricing.
What exactly is the MILO simulation range? +
MILO is a military-grade virtual simulation system used by law enforcement agencies, military units, and defense organizations worldwide. At SOTUSA, members run immersive scenario-based training — dynamic threat environments, force-on-force decision scenarios, surround sound, and real-time performance analytics. It's available to all skill levels, from first-time gun owners learning fundamentals to active military running tactical training. It's also one of the most compelling entry points for new shooters who are not yet comfortable with live fire.
Is this range open to the general public? +
No. Both the live fire range and the MILO simulation bays are exclusively reserved for members and their approved guests. You will never share your training session with walk-in public traffic — this is a private club by design, and that will not change.
Can annual members upgrade to a lifetime membership later? +
Yes. Annual club members may convert to a lifetime founding membership at any time while spots remain. A portion of dues already paid will be applied toward the one-time lifetime fee. Ask about the upgrade path and exact credit calculation when you apply — we'll walk you through it in detail.
When does the club open and what is the construction status? +
SOTUSA has been building this facility for over four years. Infrastructure is currently 90% complete, all permits and special use licenses are secured (including our live fire range operating permit issued in perpetuity), and we can be fully operational within approximately five months of funding completion. Founding members will receive a priority private walkthrough and grand opening invitation before any public announcement.
What firearms can I bring to the live fire range? +
Members may use their own personally owned firearms on the live fire range, subject to standard range safety protocols. We support rifle and pistol calibers including high-caliber applications. A curated selection of rental firearms is also available for members who prefer to shoot without bringing their own. Our range staff will walk you through protocols during your initial orientation.
What is included in the NOISHA cleanroom certification? +
Our live fire range is the first and only NOISHA-certified cleanroom indoor range in Arizona. This means we use an AAON HVAC system with HEPA 99.97% recirculation and air purification — ensuring the highest possible air quality for members and staff. We also use PEPP and acoustic baffles for sound suppression, and Quiet Range Technology with BERM Retention. This is a significant health and safety advantage over any standard indoor range.
